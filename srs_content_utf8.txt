Introduction    The Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document provides a complete description of the functional and non-functional requirements of the  Mind Ease Mental  Health Support System. This document explains what the system must do, ensuring that the designers and develope rs fully understand the systems  expected behavior. This SRS focuses only on requirements, not design or implementation details. 1.1 Purpose    The purpose of this SRS is to clearly define the requirements of the  Mind Ease Mental  Health Support System, a web-based platform designed to support users in monitoring, improving, and understanding their mental health through personalized tools such as mood tracking, self-assessment quizzes, AI-assisted chat, and therapist appointment booking. This document is intended for:  Developers and Designers: to understand system features and build the application accordingly.  Project Supervisors and Evaluators: to review the project goals, scope, and planned functionalities.  Testers: to verify that the final product meets all stated requirements.  Stakeholders and End Users: to understand what the system will provide. 1.2 Scope   The software product to be developed is titled  Mind  Ease   Mental  Health Support System. This system will:  Provide users with self-assessment quizzes to evaluate stress, anxiety, or emotional states.  Offer mood tracking features with visual insights and daily mood logs.  Enable AI-assisted chat for basic emotional support and guidance.  Allow users to book appointments with mental-health professionals.  Maintain user profiles, login authentication, and secure data handling.  Present educational resources, tips, and self-care content related to mental well-being.  This system will not:  Replace certified psychologists or offer medical diagnosis.  Provide emergency mental-health treatment.  Function as a real-time crisis management platform.  Application and Benefits:  The system aims to:  Help individuals better understand their emotional patterns.  Provide an accessible, stigma-free environment for mental wellness.  Offer AI-supported guidance for everyday emotional challenges.  Connect users with professionals when deeper support is needed.  Empower users through consistent tracking, reflection, and personalized insights.  The scope described here summarizes the overall objectives and benefits of the system without listing individual requirements, which are detailed in later sections. 1.3 Definitions , Acronyms, and Abbreviations Below are the definitions and abbreviations used within this SRS:   AI: Artificial Intelligence. Detailed technical requirements are presented later in Section 3. 2.1 Product  Perspective    The  Mind  Ease   Mental  Health Support System is an independent and self-contained  web application. It is not part of a larger system; however, it interacts with external services  such as AI-based sentiment analysis APIs (e.g., OpenAI), email services, and optional  third-party appointment scheduling tools.  The system is designed to fill a growing need in the digital mental health sector by providing emotionally supportive tools, such as:  Daily mood tracking  Self-assessment quizzes  AI-assisted chat for emotional support  Therapist appointment booking  Educational mental health resources  Comparison with Other Platforms  While existing mental health platforms (e.g.,  Better Help , Calm,  Mood fit ) offer individual  features such as therapy booking or meditation,  Mind Ease  stands out through its all-in-one  integrated platform, combining assessment tools, personalized mood insights, AI-assisted  conversation, and a smooth user experience focused on accessibility and comfort.  Unlike commercial systems,  Mind Ease  does not replace professional medical evaluation,  but supports users in tracking and understanding their emotional trends. Environment variables (API keys, DB URI, server port) must be set correctly. Self-Assessment Quizzes  Stress, anxiety, and mood quizzes help users identify emotional states.  Mood Tracking  Users record daily moods which are later visualized using graphs/trends. Appointment Booking  Users view therapist profiles and schedule online appointments.  Dashboard with Insights  Displays suggested resources, weekly mood summary, recent activity, and  recommendations.  Educational Resources  Articles, self-help guides, breathing exercises, and mental health tips.  These functions work together to create a supportive, user-friendly mental wellness platform. Therapists who join the platform manage their availability schedules.  Users provide accurate information during assessments.  Email or SMS service for notifications remains active. Specific Requirements    This section describes the detailed functional and non-functional requirements of the  Mind   Ease Mental Health Support System. This section does not describe how the system will be designed; it describes what the  system must do  3.1 External Interface Requirements 3.1.1 System Interfaces The system interacts with several external services and components. Each interface is listed  below along with its purpose and expected behavior.  3.1.1.1 AI Sentiment Analysis API  Description:  The system must integrate with an AI API (e.g., OpenAI API) to perform text-based  sentiment analysis for the AI chat and mood prediction feature.  Functionality:  Sends user-provided text and receives analyzed sentiment or AI-generated  supportive messages.  Interface:  HTTPS-based REST API  Request Format: JSON  Response Format: JSON  Purpose:  To provide personalized and adaptive emotional support.  3.1.1.2 Email/Notification Service  Description:  The system must connect with a mail service (e.g.,  Node mailer  with SMTP) to send  appointment confirmations and password reset emails.  Interface:  SMTP / Secure Email API  Purpose:  Notify users about appointments, account activities, and reminders.  3.1.1.3 Database System (MongoDB)  Description:  The system must store user data, quiz results, mood entries, chat logs, and  appointment bookings in MongoDB.  Interface:  Connection via Mongoose library (MongoDB driver).  Purpose:  Provide persistent, secure, and scalable data management. 3.1.2 Interfaces 3.1.2.1 Logical Interface Characteristics  The system will be accessed through a web-based Graphical User Interface (GUI)  developed using React.js.  Key characteristics include:  Intuitive navigation with a top Navbar and left-side Dashboard panel.  Simple, calming color theme suitable for mental health applications.  Large buttons and readable typography for accessibility.  Interactive visual elements (charts, progress bars, cards).  3.1.2.2 Interface Optimization for Users  The interface must:  Support both desktop and mobile devices (responsive design).  Present accurate error messages (invalid inputs, blank fields).  Include focus indicators for screen-reader compatibility.  Support accessible color contrast for users with visual impairment.  Use clear and friendly microcopy (â€œHow are you feeling today?â€ instead of technical language).  3.1.2.3 Interaction Modes  Users interact with forms, quizzes, charts, and chat windows.  All interactions occur through point-and-click and keyboard navigation.  No command-line interface is required. 3.1.3 Hardware Interfaces The system has no direct hardware interface requirements.  Since Mind   Ease is a web-based software application, it does not communicate with or  control any specialized hardware devices.  The only requirement is that users access the system through standard computing devices  (desktop, laptop, tablet, smartphone) with an internet connection. 3.1.4 Software Interfaces The system depends on several external software products and APIs to operate correctly.  3.1.4.1 Node.js  Mnemonic: Node  Version: LTS (16.x or later)  Source:  OpenJS  Foundation  Purpose: Backend server runtime environment.  3.1.4.2 Express.js  Mnemonic: Express  Version: Compatible with Node.js  Purpose: Provides routing and middleware support for API endpoints.  3.1.4.3 MongoDB + Mongoose  Mnemonic: MongoDB  Version: 5.0+  Source: MongoDB Inc.  Purpose: Database to store all system data.  Interface: JSON-based documents through Mongoose ORM.  3.1.4.4 React.js  Mnemonic: React  Version: 18+  Source: Meta Platforms  Purpose: Frontend UI rendering and component management.  3.1.4.5 AI Sentiment/Chat API  Mnemonic: OpenAI API / Similar  Protocol: HTTPS REST API  Purpose: AI-driven emotional support, mood prediction, and message generation.  Message Format:  Request: JSON containing user text  Response: JSON containing sentiment or response text.  3.1.4.6 Chart Library (e.g., Chart.js or Recharts)  Purpose: Display mood trends and insights in visual form.  3.1.4.7 CSS Framework (Tailwind / Custom CSS)  Purpose: Provide consistent design and responsive layouts. 3.1.5 Communications Interfaces The system relies on standard communication protocols:  HTTP/HTTPS Protocol  All requests between frontend, backend, and APIs occur through secure  HTTPS.  REST-based architecture.  TCP/IP  Required at networking level for all client-server communication.  SMTP (Email Service)  Used for account notifications and reminders. All requirements in this section are measurable, testable, and apply system-wide 3.5.1 Performance System Response Time  95% of all user requests (login, quiz loading, mood entry, chat response) shall  be processed within 1 second.  AI chat responses shall be received within 3 seconds under normal load.  Server Processing Speed  Backend API endpoints must complete processing within 500ms for standard  operations.  Page Load Time  All UI pages should load within 2 seconds over a standard broadband  connection.  Scalability  The system must support a minimum of 500 concurrent users without  performance degradation. 3.5.2 Reliability Uptime Target  The system shall maintain a reliability level of 99% uptime excluding  scheduled maintenance.  MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)  The system shall maintain a minimum MTBF of 30 days.  Fault Recovery  The system must recover from non-critical failures within 2 minutes.  Automatic reconnection to the database must occur if the connection is lost  temporarily .  3.5.3 Availability Availability Percentage  System availability must be at least 99% over a 24-hour cycle.  Downtime Window  Downtime shall not exceed 1 minute per day for automated deployments or  background tasks.  User Data Access  Users should be able to access their dashboard and personal data at all times  except during scheduled maintenance. 3.5.4 Security   Authentication & Authorization  All secure operations require JWT-based authentication.  Passwords must be hashed using industry-standard hashing algorithms ( bcrypt  or better).  Data Encryption  All communication between frontend, backend, and external APIs must use  HTTPS (TLS 1.2 or higher).  Sensitive data stored in the database must use encryption at rest.  Access Control  Only authorized users may access personal data, chat history, or  appointments .  Admin users shall have restricted admin-panel access.  Data Privacy  No user mental health data shall be shared without explicit user consent.  System must comply with basic digital privacy principles.  Input Validation  All user inputs must be validated to prevent SQL injection, XSS, CSRF, and other attacks.  3.5.5 Maintainability Code Modularity  The system shall be developed using modular components to support  updates with minimal changes.  Documentation  Every major function must include internal comments and documentation.  Error Logs  The system must maintain a structured error log for debugging and maintenance.  Dependency Updates  All third-party libraries must be updated at least once every 6 months. 3.5.6 Portability Browser Compatibility   The system must support modern browsers: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari. Appendix A.1 Mental Health Support Systems Mental health support systems are digital platforms designed to assist users in managing stress, anxiety, depression, and emotional well-being. Traditional mental health services face challenges such as limited accessibility, social stigma, and high costs. Digital platforms help overcome these barriers by providing: 24/7 accessibility Anonymity and privacy Self-help tools and guided support Early detection of mental health issues Recent studies show that web-based mental health platforms improve user engagement and provide effective preliminary support, especially for young adults and students. Appendix A.2 Artificial Intelligence in Mental Health Artificial Intelligence (AI) plays a significant role in modern mental health applications. AI techniques such as Natural Language Processing (NLP) and sentiment analysis enable systems to: Analyze user text to detect emotional states Provide personalized mental health feedback Predict mood patterns over time Assist users through AI-powered chat support AI does not replace professional therapists but serves as a supportive tool that enhances early intervention and self-awareness. Appendix A.3 Mood Tracking and Behavioral Analytics Mood tracking allows users to log their daily emotional states and observe long-term behavioral patterns. By analyzing mood data over time, systems can generate insights such as: Emotional trends Stress triggers Improvement or deterioration patterns When combined with AI-based analytics, mood tracking helps users better understand their mental health and  encourages proactive self-care.
